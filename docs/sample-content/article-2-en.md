Uncertainty Budgets in Medical Device GD&T: A Practical Guide
February 27, 2026 · White Paper · 8 min read

This white paper addresses one of the most persistent challenges in FDA-regulated medical device manufacturing: building uncertainty budgets for geometric dimensioning and tolerancing (GD&T) that satisfy both ISO 14253-1 and FDA 21 CFR Part 11 audit requirements.

Drawing on over a decade of deployment experience across Class II and Class III device manufacturers, our metrology team presents a structured, step-by-step methodology for identifying, quantifying, and documenting all significant sources of measurement uncertainty — from operator variability and fixture compliance to environmental temperature gradients.

What You Will Learn
The five-category uncertainty budget framework (Type A and Type B sources)
How to calculate the expanded uncertainty U95 for surface profile and true position callouts
Gauge R&R study design for CMM-based GD&T measurement systems
Documentation templates aligned with ISO/IEC 17025 and FDA audit expectations
